California state preemption

Updated: Jan 2025 (CA HCD ADU Handbook). Always check your city’s ADU ordinance for objective local standards.

Max size
Must allow at least 800 sf notwithstanding FAR/lot coverage. Also at least 850 sf (studio/1-BR) or 1,000 sf (2+ BR).
Height
Detached: 16 ft baseline; 18 ft within ½-mile of transit or on lots with multifamily; +2 ft for matching roof pitch. Attached: up to 25 ft or primary height (lower).
Setbacks
At least 4 ft side & rear for new detached ADUs.
Parking
Max 1 space per unit/bedroom; exempt within ½-mile of transit, historic districts, conversions/within primary, when permits aren’t offered, or when car-share within 1 block.
Owner-occupancy
Prohibited for ADUs (no owner-occ requirement).
Impact fees
None for ADUs < 750 sf; otherwise proportional to ADU vs. primary size.

Oregon state rules

Updated: September 2, 2025. Oregon law (ORS 197.312(5), as updated by HB 2001) requires cities ≥ 2,500 and counties ≥ 15,000 within urban growth boundaries (UGBs) to allow at least one ADU per detached single‑family dwelling, subject to clear, objective siting/design rules. Local codes may not require owner‑occupancy or additional off‑street parking for ADUs, with limited exceptions for vacation rentals.

Max size
Set locally; common patterns around 800–900 sf or 75–85% of the primary home. See your city’s portal.
Height
Set locally; often ~20–25 ft under accessory‑building standards. Confirm per zone/overlay.
Setbacks
Set locally; rear/side commonly ~4–5 ft depending on zoning. Check your parcel.
Parking
Not allowed as a condition of ADU approval (limited exceptions for vacation rentals). HB 2001 FAQ
Owner‑occupancy
Not allowed as a local requirement for ADUs (limited exceptions for vacation rentals). HB 2001 FAQ
Impact fees
Some cities waive/reduce SDCs; e.g., Portland’s ADU SDC Waiver requires a 10‑year covenant (no STRs during the term). Portland SDC Waiver

Statewide legal basis: DLCD ADU Guidance • ORS 197.312(5) • LOC summary of Kamps‑Hughes v. Eugene • Building code: ORSC.

Washington state preemption — RCW 36.70A.680–.681

State law is in effect in GMA urban growth areas. If a city/county hasn’t updated yet, the statewide standards apply.

How many
At least two ADUs per lot (any mix of attached/detached) where single-family homes are allowed in UGAs.
Min size
Local codes may not require ADUs smaller than 1,000 sf.
Height & design parity
ADUs can’t have stricter setbacks/coverage/design than the principal; height may not be limited below 24 ft or the principal’s height limit.
Parking
No on-site parking may be required within ½ mile of a major transit stop; otherwise max 1 space/unit on lots < 6,000 sf and 2 spaces/unit on lots > 6,000 sf.
Owner-occupancy
Not required for the principal home or ADUs (limited STR exceptions may apply).
Fees & improvements
Impact fees capped at ≤ 50% of the principal unit; no public street improvements may be required as a permit condition.

Colorado new statewide baseline (2025)

HB24‑1152 requires many metro jurisdictions to allow one ADU by‑right with administrative approval by June 30, 2025, and limits certain local restrictions.

Where it applies
“Subject jurisdictions” (specified cities/counties in MPOs and large CDPs).
Allowance
One ADU accessory to a single‑unit detached dwelling by‑right via ministerial/administrative review.
Local limits
Bill restricts various local prohibitions and encourages pre‑approved plans and fee reductions via grants.
Timeline
Effective and signed May 13, 2024; compliance and reporting in 2025.

Verify: Outside “subject jurisdictions,” rules remain local. Fire/wildland overlays and HOAs may add standards.

Texas local control (state bills pending)

As of mid‑2025, there is no uniform statewide ADU preemption. Several statewide bills have been introduced to standardize ADU allowances; check current status. City codes (e.g., Austin, Dallas, Houston) govern today.

Max size/height
City‑specific (often 550–1,100 sf limits; heights 15–25 ft typical).
Parking
City‑specific (common: 1 off‑street space for ADU unless waived near transit).
Owner‑occupancy
Varies by city; some removed requirements, others retain.
Status watch
2025 session considered ADU preemption bills (e.g., SB 673/HB 2480). Confirm enactment before relying.

Verify: Always read your city’s latest ordinance and any design/parking overlays.

New York local control (+ NYC Appendix U)

No statewide ADU legalization as of 2025. The state is pursuing incentives; NYC adopted Building Code Appendix U (Local Law 127 of 2024) to pilot/enable ADUs with safety requirements.

State status
Incentive bill activity (forgivable loan program proposals); no uniform preemption.
NYC
Appendix U establishes technical and safety standards for ADUs (egress, sprinklers/windows, flood measures), with DOB guidance.
Elsewhere
ADU feasibility varies by municipality; check local zoning and building codes.
Parking/owner‑occ
Local.

Verify: NYC rules are evolving; borough‑specific pilots and DOB bulletins govern implementation.

How to get your ADU permitted (fast)

  1. Confirm eligibility: Lot zoning, overlays, wildfire/shoreline, utilities.
  2. Pick a compliant footprint: Choose a size/height that fits the statewide minimums and your city’s objective standards.
  3. Speed up design: Use pre‑approved plans (if available) or a builder’s plan set with engineering.
  4. Ministerial submittal: Apply for administrative review with a complete checklist; target 30–60 day approvals.
  5. Inspections & utilities: Schedule foundation/rough/final; coordinate tie‑ins and any panel upgrades.

ADU regulations: quick FAQ

Do I need off‑street parking for an ADU?

In many states (e.g., CA statewide; many WA jurisdictions), parking is waived near transit or for conversions. In others, it’s city‑specific.

Can cities still require me to live on‑site?

California prohibits owner‑occupancy requirements for ADUs. Oregon also disallows them within UGBs. Washington’s HB 1337 removes them in covered jurisdictions. Elsewhere varies.

What if my city’s website conflicts with state law?

State law usually preempts. Bring the statute/handbook to planning or request a supervisor review. We’ve linked official sources and can help escalate.

How big can my ADU be?

California guarantees up to 800 sf regardless of lot limits; at least 850/1,000 sf by bedrooms. Washington caps minimum size standards at 1,000 sf. Oregon leaves size to cities. Others vary.

Are impact fees waived?

CA waives impact fees for ADUs under 750 sf; WA caps some fees. In OR/CO/TX/NY, fees are local.
