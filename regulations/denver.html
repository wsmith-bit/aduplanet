Denver 6-point rule card

Max size
Set by zone/lot and structure type; common caps around ~650–864+ sf or tied to lot/primary ratios. {{verify locally}}
Height
Typically ~16–24 ft depending on zone and roof form; alley context may affect. {{verify locally}}
Setbacks
Detached ADUs follow accessory structure setbacks; rear/side often smaller than primary. Corner and alley lots vary. {{verify locally}}
Parking
Standards vary by zone and proximity to transit; designation of an existing space may be required; new off-street may not be required in some contexts. {{verify locally}}
Owner-occupancy
Denver does not generally require ongoing owner-occ for ADUs; confirm any registration/licensing conditions. {{verify locally}}
Design/entries
Facade/entry placement and window privacy rules can apply, especially on alley-loaded lots or two-story forms. {{verify locally}}

Note: Denver zoning updates have expanded ADU allowances in more neighborhoods. Always check your address and zone district in the city tool to confirm current rules. {{verify locally}}

Permit HowTo (Denver flow)

  1. Confirm your zone & coverage: Look up your address in Denver’s zoning map; confirm ADU allowance and any neighborhood overlays. {{verify locally}}
  2. Pre-design & site plan: Choose detached vs. garage conversion vs. attached. Sketch footprint, setbacks, access, and utilities.
  3. Zoning review (objective): Submit site plan, elevations, and any required forms. Many reviews are administrative if standards are met.
  4. Building permit: Submit construction drawings; meet Denver Building & Fire code (egress, energy, structural). Contractor licensing or homeowner-builder rules apply. {{verify locally}}
  5. Utilities & addressing: Coordinate water/sewer and power; separate meters vary by project; address assignment is common. {{verify locally}}
  6. Inspections & final: Expect multiple inspections; close permits for C/O or final. Register rental if applicable. {{verify locally}}

Indicative timelines: zoning review in weeks when plans are complete; building review varies by queue and completeness. {{verify locally}}

Denver ADU FAQ

Are ADUs allowed on my lot?

Check your address in the Denver zoning map to confirm your zone and whether ADUs are permitted. Some districts allow by right; others may not. {{verify locally}}

What size ADU can I build?

Size depends on zone, lot, and structure type. Common detached caps land around ~650–864+ sf, but confirm your district’s standard. {{verify locally}}

Do I need off-street parking?

Often you can designate an existing space; new spaces may not be required in some contexts, especially near transit, but rules vary by zone. {{verify locally}}

Is owner-occupancy required?

Ongoing owner-occupancy is generally not required; verify any registration/licensing conditions. {{verify locally}}

Garage conversion vs. new detached?

Conversions can lower costs/time if structure is viable; detached units offer more design freedom but must meet setbacks and height limits. {{verify locally}}
