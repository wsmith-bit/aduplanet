How to get an ADU permit in Portland (5 steps)

  1. Check your zoning — Use Portland Maps / zoning lookup to confirm ADUs are allowed on your lot and note overlays. {{verify locally}}
  2. Draft your plans — Site, floor, elevations; match form standards (height, setbacks, entries, roof pitch). {{verify locally}}
  3. Pre-application guidance — Optional coaching/early assistance with BDS to de-risk corrections. {{verify locally}}
  4. Submit permits — Planning/Zoning + Building + (as needed) Site/Utilities, Tree, and any overlay reviews. {{verify locally}}
  5. Build & inspections — Schedule inspections per City sequence; obtain final and certificate of occupancy. {{verify locally}}

Target review windows may vary with workload; plan contingencies into your schedule.

Portland ADU FAQs

Is owner-occupancy required for ADUs in Portland?

No. Oregon removed owner-occupancy requirements for ADUs statewide in 2021. Verify if any local nuances remain. {{verify locally}}

What’s the maximum ADU size in Portland?

Expect ~800–1,000 sf depending on zone, lot coverage, and design standards. Always confirm your exact cap with current code. {{verify locally}}

Do I need to provide off-street parking?

Often no—especially near transit—but requirements depend on zoning and site context. Confirm during pre-app. {{verify locally}}

Are SDC fees waived for ADUs?

Portland has periodically offered SDC waivers/reductions for ADUs; these programs change. Check current status before budgeting. {{verify locally}}

How long does permitting take?

Timelines vary; state encourages ~60 days, but local workloads differ. Ask BDS for current estimates. {{verify locally}}

Can I build a detached ADU in a back yard with trees?

Possibly, but tree preservation and root protection zones can affect placement and foundation choices. Coordinate with Urban Forestry / BDS. {{verify locally}}
