Oregon ADU Regulations & Permits (2025)

Statewide ADU laws simplify building in Oregon cities, but local rules still matter. Here’s what you need to know.

TL;DR: Oregon requires most cities to allow ADUs, but local size, height, and design standards differ. No owner-occupancy rule applies, and some cities waive or reduce fees. Always check your local planning portal for the latest updates.

How to Get an ADU Permit in Oregon

  1. Confirm zoning: Verify your lot allows ADUs under city code.
  2. Prepare plans: Submit site + floor plans that meet local standards.
  3. Apply for permits: File through your city’s planning/building department.
  4. Plan review: City reviews compliance (target 60 days, varies).
  5. Inspections & approval: Build, schedule inspections, and obtain occupancy certificate.

Oregon ADU FAQs

Do I need to live on-site if I build an ADU in Oregon?

No. Since 2021, Oregon law prohibits owner-occupancy requirements for ADUs. {{verify locally}}

Can cities still limit ADU size?

Yes, cities may set maximums (commonly 800–1,000 sf). Always confirm with your local ordinance. {{verify locally}}

Are parking spaces required?

Often not, especially near transit or for conversions. {{verify locally}}

How long does ADU permitting take in Oregon?

State law encourages faster review (~60 days), but local backlogs may extend timelines. {{verify locally}}

Does Oregon offer ADU fee waivers?

Some cities (like Portland) waive or reduce system development charges (SDCs). {{verify locally}}
