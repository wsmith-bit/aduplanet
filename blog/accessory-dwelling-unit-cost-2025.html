What drives ADU cost

  • Size & shape: Compact rectangles cost less per square foot than complex footprints.
  • Foundation: Slab vs raised; soils & drainage can require upgrades {{verify locally}}.
  • Utilities distance: Long trench runs, panel upsizing, or sewer laterals add cost.
  • Structure & roof: Simpler spans and standard roof pitches reduce engineering and labor.
  • Finish level: Cabinets, tile, windows, and fixtures swing totals quickly.
  • Permits & fees: Impact/utility fees vary widely by city/county {{verify locally}}.
  • Labor market: High-demand regions command higher bids.

Ways to save (without regrets)

  • Right-size the plan (e.g., 600–800 sf hits a sweet spot for cost-per-function).
  • Favor a simple rectangle, standard spans, and efficient roof geometry.
  • Minimize plumbing “wet wall” runs; stack kitchen and bath back-to-back.
  • Use mid-grade finishes where durability matters; avoid over-customization.
  • Consider prefab or panelized to shorten schedule and reduce change orders.
  • Check utility capacity early (panel size, sewer lateral) {{verify locally}}.
  • Bid apples-to-apples with a clear scope; include contingency (5–10%).

ADU cost FAQ

What’s the cost per square foot for an ADU?
Many projects land around $275–$525/sf for hard costs, depending on type and market. Very small units can have a higher $/sf due to fixed costs.
Are permits and impact fees included in the ranges?
Ranges include typical soft costs, but impact and utility fees vary by city/county. Check the official fee schedule {{verify locally}}.
Do prefab prices include foundation and hookups?
Usually not. Budget for foundation, crane (if needed), and utility trenching. Always confirm the “installed” scope in writing.
How quickly can I build?
Design & permits: ~2–4 months; build: ~4–8 months. Prefab/panelized can shorten the on-site portion.
What’s a typical payback period from rent?
With market rent and stable occupancy, many projects pay back in ~8–15 years. Taxes, financing, and vacancy matter—model scenarios carefully.
