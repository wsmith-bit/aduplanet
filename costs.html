Budgets cluster around a few big buckets: the shell (foundation, framing, roof, windows), mechanical/electrical/plumbing, site work and utility trenching, interior finishes, and soft costs for design, engineering, and permits. Price swings come from site complexity (slopes, utility distance), finishes and fixtures, and regional labor markets.

  • Structure & envelope: framing, sheathing, roofing, windows/doors, insulation, drywall.
  • MEP systems: HVAC (often mini-split), electrical panel/upgrades, wiring, plumbing rough & fixtures.
  • Sitework & utilities: grading, foundation, trenching, tie-ins, driveway/walks.
  • Interior finishes: cabinets, flooring, tile, paint, trim, lighting.
  • Design & permits: architectural, structural, Title 24/energy, permits/fees.

Top cost drivers in plain English

Site complexity
Slopes, trees, soil, long utility runs
Local labor & code
Market rates + stricter energy/seismic
Size & layout
Kitchens/baths drive cost per sf

Two ADUs with the same square footage can price very differently. Short, straight utility runs and a simple roofline can save five figures. Adding a second bathroom adds far more cost per sf than adding bedroom area.

Ways to save without regret

  • Right-size the footprint: keep it compact but functional; prioritize daylight and storage.
  • Standardize: choose a plan that avoids structural gymnastics; keep roof lines simple.
  • Utilities: site the ADU close to existing sewer/water/electric to shorten trenching.
  • Spec smart: durable mid-grade finishes often outperform premium for rentals.
  • Prefab: can cut timeline and weather risk, but still budget for foundation and hookups.

ADU cost FAQ

What’s the cheapest type of ADU?
Garage conversions usually win on cost because the shell already exists. Expect $90k–$190k for 350–600 sf in typical markets.
Is prefab actually cheaper?
Prefab can cut timeline and some onsite labor risk, but you still pay for foundation, craning, and utility tie-ins. Net costs are often similar to site-built for comparable specs, with more predictability.
How accurate is the calculator?
It’s a ballpark using regional multipliers and typical category splits. For a guaranteed number, submit your address for a site-calibrated quote.
Can I build an ADU for under $100k?
Possibly for small garage conversions or in value regions with minimal upgrades, but most projects exceed $100k once design, permits, utilities, and finishes are included.
What adds the most cost per square foot?
Bathrooms and kitchens (plumbing + finishes), long utility trenches, complex foundations, and high-end finish packages move the needle fastest.
How long does permitting take?
Commonly 4–12 weeks depending on the city/county. Jurisdictions with ADU streamlining tend to be faster.
Do ADUs increase property taxes?
Usually yes, but only on the improvement value (not a full reassessment in many states). Ask your local assessor for specifics.
