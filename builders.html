Prefab vs Custom ADU Builders

Both can deliver a great ADU. Prefab typically trades customization for speed and predictability; custom optimizes fit and finishes but needs tighter project management.

FactorPrefab (modules/panelized)Custom (design‑build/GC)
SpeedFaster: factory + parallel sitework (as low as 3–6 months)Slower: sequential trades (6–12+ months)
Price certaintyHigher: bundled scope, fewer change ordersVaries: allowances, site unknowns can add cost
CustomizationLimited plans & finish packagesHigh flexibility in layout and finishes
Site constraintsCranes/clearances required for modulesWorks on tight/complex lots with staging
PermittingOften handled by builder; state approvals may helpHandled by architect/GC; city‑by‑city specifics
UtilitiesCoordinated with set & hookup crewsCoordinated through subs across phases

Tip: ask each bidder to include site work, utility trenching, and permit fees in their total so quotes are comparable.

Builder Types Directory

Prefab Modules

Fully built boxes craned into place. Fastest install.

  • Factory‑built; minimal onsite framing
  • Predictable schedule & scope
  • Requires crane access & staging

Panelized Systems

Wall/roof panels shipped flat; assembled onsite.

  • Lower transport complexity than modules
  • More flexible plans than full prefab
  • Weather window needed for assembly

Design‑Build Firms

One team from design through construction.

  • Single contract; tighter cost control
  • Custom plans and finishes
  • Timeline depends on local approvals

General Contractors (GC)

Hire architect + GC; maximum flexibility.

  • Bid each scope for best pricing
  • Strong owner oversight required
  • Schedule varies by trades availability

How to Choose the Best ADU Builder

Shortlist (week 1)

  • Gather zoning & utility basics (lot size, sewer, electrical panel).
  • Pick 2–3 builder types to compare (prefab, design‑build, GC).
  • Define target size & budget band (e.g., 450–800 sf, $$–$$$).
  • Ask for recent ADU references within your city.

Bid apples‑to‑apples (week 2)

  • Require line items for site work, trenching, and utility upgrades.
  • Confirm permit handling and timeline assumptions.
  • Request allowances for finishes and appliances.
  • Clarify exclusions (landscaping, solar, impact fees).

Builder FAQs

Are prefab ADUs worth it compared to custom?
Prefab can reduce build time and change‑order risk. Custom wins on flexibility and finishes. Request both quotes to see the tradeoffs on your lot.
What’s the difference between prefab, modular, and panelized?
Modular = fully built boxes; panelized = flat‑packed panels; both are forms of prefab. Modular is fastest to set; panelized offers more onsite flexibility.
Do builders handle permits?
Many prefab and design‑build firms manage permits end‑to‑end. With a GC route, your architect typically leads permitting.
How many bids should I get?
Three is ideal: one prefab, one design‑build, and one GC—so you can compare scope, timeline, and site costs fairly.
Will I need utility upgrades?
Possibly. Common items are electrical service, sewer capacity, and water line sizing. Ask bidders to include allowances for upgrades.
Can I finance builder payments?
Yes—HELOCs, construction loans, and cash‑out refis are common. See our Financing guide for options.
